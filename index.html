About Me

Ever since I built my first prototype of a low-cost prosthetic hand for a school project, I have been captivated by the power of engineering to turn ideas into tools that support others. As captain of Lincoln High School’s robotics team, I leverage Python programming, 3D printing, and hands-on robotics knowledge to lead collaborative design work. I rely on strong problem-solving, public speaking, and teamwork to guide my teammates, organize projects, and share our work with audiences.

Beyond engineering, I carry this service-focused mindset into daily life. As a varsity cross-country runner, I cultivate resilience and consistent discipline; as a community library math tutor, I enjoy breaking down complex concepts for younger learners. Winning first place at the regional science fair for my affordable prosthetics project strengthened my ambition to pursue biomedical engineering. I aim to design accessible, low-cost medical devices to reduce barriers to healthcare for communities in need.
